Investigation Overview¶

In this study, I want to look at the aspects of loans that could influence or forecast a borrower's APR. In addition to the APR, my major characteristic of interest, I'm also interested in how other variables might impact the APR's relationship with other variables. APR vs. loan amount and APR vs. rating are a couple of the relationships I looked at, as well as the impact of the term on the link between APR and rating. I am particularly interested in the category variables "LoanStatus," "IncomeRange," "EmploymentStatus," "Term," and "ProsperRating." I'm particularly interested in the numerical variables "BorrowerAPR," "StatedMonthlyIncome," "Loan Original Amount," and "Debt to Income Ratio."

Dataset Overview¶

The 113,937 loans in this dataset have an average of 81 variables per loan, including loan amount, borrower rate, current loan status, and borrower income.

BorrowAPR Distribution¶

The distribution appears to be multimodal, one smaller at 0.16 and one larger at 0.2. After that, it begins to decline with a 0.28 peak and a 0.35 shockwave. Almost no loans are above 0.425.

Effect of LoanAmount on BorrowerAPR¶

From the plot below, it can be seen as the amount of loan increases, there's a decrease in Borrower rating i.e the lower the amount of loan collected, the higher the rating. This explains the negative relationship obtained from our heatmap earlier

Effect of StatedMonthlyIncome on BorrowerAPR¶

From the plot below, it can as well be seen that an inverse relationship between BorrowerAPR and StatedMonthlyIncome exist i.e the higher our stated income, the lesser our borrower rating. In essence, it can be concluded that this loan favours high income earners more.

BorrowerAPR across LoanStatus and LoanOriginalAmount¶

It appears that the relationship between the initial loan amount and APR is impacted in some way by a LoanStatus. We can observe that the association between borrowers who have either repaid their loans in full or are doing so right now and defaulters and past-due accounts is greater.

BorrowerAPR across Term and IncomeRange¶

The APR rises as the income range narrows for each particular period across each bracket. Usually, the APR goes down as the period lengthens, however in this case, it goes up when the income range drops from 100,000 to zero.
